Stay Safe, Stay Home

See a doctor if you feel unwell (fever, difficulty in breathing or cough). While visiting a doctor wear mask to cover your mouth and nose. Avoid participating in large gatherings such as Marriages, Birthday Parties, Public Shows and Rallies.

Take step to protect yourself

Wear a face Mask

When you wear a mask, you protect others as well as yourself. Masks work best when everyone wears one. A mask is NOT a substitute for social distancing. Masks should still be worn in addition to staying at least 6 feet apart, especially when indoors around people who don’t live in your household.

Wash your hands

Practice frequent hand washing. Wash hands with soap and water or use alcohol based hand rub. Wash hands even if they are visibly clean. Avoid shaking hands with people as greeting gesture. You can instead use the Traditional Indian way of gretting with NAMASTE.

What are the main Symptoms?

People with COVID-19 have had a wide range of symptoms reported – ranging from mild symptoms to severe illness. Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. Anyone can have mild to severe symptoms. People with these symptoms may have COVID-19

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Sore throat
  • Nausea or vomiting
